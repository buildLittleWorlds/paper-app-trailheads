Session 5 · From surprise to direction

Your R-pentomino

The traveling question: what small thing in your world has consequences nobody can predict — and what next move does it hand you?
The bridge from class

In this week's Instructor Study page, five cells sit on a board and nobody — not even Conway — could predict their fate: the R-pentomino churns for 1,103 generations before it settles. Richard Guy watched it and found the glider; a $50 wager later, Gosper's gun turned one surprise into a machine. Then the triage forge sorts curiosities by a single test: does it hand you a next move?

That's what this page sends into your territory: a useful curiosity gives you a next move. Every lane below has a small thing with unpredictable consequences — find yours.

Pick a lane, or wander

Trailheads

Every card is a question you could chase. Try it is the smallest version — buildable tonight. Where it could go is the same question weeks deeper: branches to explore once the small version works (tags name the session that hands you the tool). Two prompts to copy: try-it builds the small version now; branch has the AI interview you and propose your version before writing any code. Chips mark the ladder: wonder → watch → compare. Your app is its own lane — those cards do not hand you a curiosity, they send you back into something you already built to go looking for one.

Start from what you already built

The Bench

Every card above starts with somebody else's curiosity. This starts with yours — the app already sitting on your GitHub page. You are going to find one thing in it you chose almost without thinking, change that one thing, and see what happens. That is the whole move, and it is the same move real researchers make.

2 Find a knob. Fill in any that apply — you do not need all five.

A knob is anything you picked but could have picked differently.

3 Pick one and guess what happens.

If I changed it, I think would happen — but I don't actually know.

Guessing wrong is the good outcome. If you already knew, there would be nothing to find out.


      


      
Nothing to put in box 2? Open your app's code and read the first ten lines. The first number you find is a knob. If there genuinely isn't one, that itself is the finding — your app has no dials, and giving it one is this week's build.

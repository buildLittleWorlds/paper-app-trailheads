Made by many hands

The traveling question: pick something made by many hands — who gets to claim it, and what would an honest credits page say?
The bridge from class

In this week's Instructor Study page, a glider loops while you answer four claims: who invented the rule, who found the glider, who built the gun, who made this one move on your screen just now? Conway wrote the rule; Richard Guy spotted the glider the way a naturalist spots a beetle; Gosper's team engineered the gun to win a fifty-dollar wager — and the one on screen ran because you pressed the button. The field never crowned one author; it named contributions. Then the note forge turns a build log into an authorship note — and flags the note that apologizes instead of taking responsibility.

That's what this page sends into your territory: authorship is a credits page, not a single name. Everything you love was made by many hands — and your app was too.

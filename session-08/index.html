The bridge from class

In this week's Instructor Study page, a random soup burns down to ash — anonymous debris, until the field guide switches on and the same pixels become blocks, blinkers, and beehives. Nothing changed on the board; everything changed in the room. And across soups, the census keeps finding the same few citizens: the ash is the false mean of the Life universe.

That's what this page sends into your territory: names make patterns visible — and a page should claim only what it shows. Every lane below has a census waiting for a field guide.

      Honesty note. Every card on this page was authored by your instructor; the Dealer's shuffles happen randomly in your browser; the copy buttons write to your clipboard and nowhere else. Nothing you click is recorded, collected, or sent anywhere. And one rule travels with every analogy here, the same one the course keeps for cellular automata and transformers: analogies find questions, not answers. Your feeder's birds are not the world's birds; a week's census is an anecdote with a tally. The idea travels; the mechanism doesn't.

Four moves

How to wander

1 Open this week's page and tap your lane — or a lane that isn't yours.
2 Read spark questions — or keep hitting the Dealer — until one makes you want to see the answer.
3 Copy a prompt: the starter (smallest version) or the bigger build if you're feeling ambitious.
4 Build, notice one thing, ship the URL. Say what you'd try next.
The fine print

Standing rules

Invitations, not assignments. No card is required. Nothing here is collected, scored, or turned in.
Borrow the interaction, not the topic. Any card's reader action can carry a completely different subject — that's the point.
Analogies find questions, not answers. Your playlist is not a transformer. The idea travels; the mechanism doesn't.
The smallest version wins. One page, one working interaction, one thing to notice beats a big plan.
Wondering what a finished trail looks like? The Instructor Study holds the week's demonstration, and the Anthology is the museum of interactions worth borrowing.

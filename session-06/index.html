Session 6 · Evaluation and robustness

Three tests of “it works”

The traveling question: pick something you love — what are three different tests of “it works,” and which one is hardest to pass?
The bridge from class

In this week's Instructor Study page, a real glider gun passes two tests — reach the target once, keep working — and then a reader with a damage brush runs the third, and it fails: nothing ever trained the gun to survive a reader. Beside it, one slider grades the same skill two ways and gets two different stories — a sudden cliff or a smooth slope. The yardstick can manufacture the jump.

That's the split this page sends into your territory: success depends on which behavior you test. Everything you love has three tests waiting.

Pick a lane, or wander

Trailheads

Every card is a question you could chase. Try it is the smallest version — buildable tonight. Where it could go is the same question weeks deeper: branches to explore once the small version works (tags name the session that hands you the tool). Two prompts to copy: try-it builds the small version now; branch has the AI interview you and propose your version before writing any code. Chips mark the ladder: wonder → watch → compare. Your app is its own lane — those cards do not hand you a curiosity, they send you back into something you already built to go looking for one.

Three tests of your own app

The Bench

Every card above tests somebody else's thing. This one tests yours — the app already sitting on your GitHub page. "It works" is three different claims. Write what each one means for your app, pick the one you are least sure of, and run it tonight — for real, with the result on the page.

2 Write the three tests for YOUR app. Fill in any — you do not need all three.

The same app can pass one and fail another. That gap is the finding.

3 Pick the one you are least sure of, and call it.

I think my app would it, because — but I don't actually know.

Calling it wrong is the good outcome. If you already knew, there would be nothing to find out.


      


      
Can't name a test? Try to break your app for one minute. Whatever you did first IS your survives-trouble test. And if you can't break it in a minute, that goes on the page too — dated, not bragged.
